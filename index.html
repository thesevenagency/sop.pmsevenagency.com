Part 1: The Core Principles Handbook

Version 1.0 | Last Updated: 1 July 2025

Welcome to the 7.agency team. Whether you are a full-time team member or a trusted vendor partner, you are the face of our company on a client's most important day. This handbook outlines the non-negotiable core principles that ensure we deliver the exceptional experience our clients expect. Adherence to these standards is mandatory for everyone working at one of our events.

1. The Client Promise: Our Guiding Star

Everything we do is centered around creating a perfect, seamless, and unforgettable wedding day for our clients. The Project Manager (PM) is the lead coordinator, responsible for every detail and for solving any issue that may arise. Our collective goal is to work together as a single, coordinated team to flawlessly execute the client's vision.

2. Universal Code of Conduct

These principles are the minimum standard of behavior and presentation expected from every individual on-site.

a. Professional Appearance & Hygiene

  • Be Presentable: Arrive in a clean, ironed, and appropriate uniform or attire as specified for the event. Personal hygiene is essential; you must be well-groomed and smell fresh.
  • Appropriate Attire: Unless a specific uniform is required, clothing should be professional and conservative. Ripped jeans, shorts, and casual T-shirts with large prints are not permitted.
  • Footwear: Shoes must be clean and in good condition. Closed-toe shoes are required for most roles unless otherwise specified for beach events.

b. Positive & Professional Attitude

  • Be Client-Focused: Your primary focus is the client's experience. Be friendly, positive, and always willing to help.
  • Be Solution-Oriented: If a challenge arises, focus on finding a solution. If you cannot solve it yourself, you must immediately report it to the on-site Project Manager.
  • Be a Team Player: We succeed as a team. Be proactive and offer help to colleagues when you see a need. An "it's not my job" attitude is not acceptable.

c. On-Site Behavior & Etiquette

  • Respect the Venue: Keep the event space, both front-of-house and back-of-house, clean and tidy. All personal items and equipment should be stored discreetly and out of sight of the clients and their guests.
  • Respect Client Privacy: An event is a private, intimate occasion. Do not allow strangers to enter the event space. Politely ask onlookers to keep their distance.
  • Discreet Conduct: Do not eat, smoke, or take personal phone calls in any area visible to clients or guests. Use designated staff areas only. Keep conversations with colleagues professional and at a low volume.
  • Punctuality: Arrive at the time specified in the rundown. Late arrivals are not acceptable.

3. Handling On-Site Challenges

Non-standard situations can occur. When they do, we respond with calm professionalism.

  • Your First Responsibility: Your primary contact for any problem, question, or deviation from the plan is the Project Manager (PM).
  • Communicate Immediately: Do not wait for a problem to escalate. Inform the PM as soon as you become aware of an issue, no matter how small it seems. This allows the leadership team to solve it effectively.

4. Our Commitment to Quality

These principles are the foundation of our reputation. They are not optional.

  • Accountability: Every team member and vendor partner is held accountable for upholding these standards.
  • Consequences: Failure to adhere to this Code of Conduct will be addressed by the Project Manager and may result in penalties, including removal from the event and exclusion from future projects.

Thank you for your professionalism and your commitment to making every event a perfect experience.

Part 3: Vendor Partnership Guide

Version 1.3 | Last Updated: 1 July 2025

Welcome, and thank you for partnering with 7.agency. We consider our vendors to be an essential part of our team, crucial to creating the perfect, seamless, and unforgettable events our clients expect. This guide outlines our quality standards and on-site procedures. Your adherence to these guidelines ensures a smooth collaboration and a flawless event for our mutual client.

Section A: Universal Standards of Partnership

These core principles are the foundation of our work and are mandatory for every person on-site.

  1. The Client-First Principle: Our collective goal is to execute the client's vision perfectly. We work as one unified team, led by the 7.agency Project Manager (PM), who is the final authority on-site.
  2. Professional Appearance: All team members must arrive in a clean, ironed, and appropriate uniform or attire. If you do not have a company uniform, plain black shirts and black trousers/skirts are required. All clothing must be presentable, and footwear must be clean and closed-toe (unless specified otherwise for beach events).
  3. Professional Attitude: We expect a positive, friendly, and solution-oriented attitude at all times. Be proactive, helpful, and focused on delivering the highest quality service.
  4. On-Site Etiquette:
    • Punctuality: Arrive at the time specified in the rundown. Late arrivals are not acceptable.
    • Discretion: All equipment and personal items must be kept out of sight of clients. Do not eat, smoke, or take personal phone calls in guest areas. Use designated staff areas only.
    • Communication: Your primary point of contact on-site is the assigned 7.agency Project Manager (PM) or Area Manager (AM). Report any issues or questions to them immediately. Do not promise changes or extras to the client directly.

Section B: Service-Specific Quality Standards

Please find the detailed requirements for your specific service area below.

B1. Photography

  • Arrival: Arrive at least 15 minutes before your scheduled start time.
  • Team: It is recommended you work with assistants, depending on the event size.
  • Client Direction: You must be able to direct the couple in English, manage the photoshoots, and follow the shot list and timeline provided by the PM.
  • Mandatory Shot List (Minimum): Details: Bride's & Groom's attire and accessories, bouquets, boutonnieres, rings. People: Bridal party, groomsmen, and family preparations and portraits. Decor: Detailed shots of the ceremony and reception setup before guests arrive. Moments: Ceremony procession, vows, ring exchange, kiss, flower shower, reception entrance, cake cutting, first dance, speeches.
  • Deliverables: All edited photos must be delivered to 7.agency within 2 weeks of the event date. The number of edited photos will be specified per your booking agreement.

Section C: Booking & Payment

  • Booking: All official bookings and changes will be communicated in writing from our 7agency.bookings@gmail.com email address.
  • Payment: All payments are processed via invoice. After the event, please submit an invoice for your services. Our Finance Manager will process the payment, which will be issued on the next business day after the event or upon receipt of the invoice.
  • ID Cards: Please ensure all your on-site staff bring their ID cards for venue security access.

We look forward to a successful partnership and creating another beautiful event together.

Part 2: Internal Team Operations Manual

Audience: For 7.agency Internal Team Members Only

This manual is the comprehensive guide to our internal operations. It details your roles, our standard procedures from booking to event completion, and our internal policies. Mastering this document is essential for the successful execution of every event and for your professional growth within the agency.

Section A: Team Roles & Responsibilities

1. Project Manager (PM)

The PM is the leader of the event. They are responsible for the entire project lifecycle and have ultimate authority on-site.

Responsibilities:

  • Oversee the event from initial booking to final report.
  • Ensure all vendor bookings are correct and confirmed.
  • Create and manage the event rundown and on-site layout.
  • Lead the entire on-site team (internal and vendor).
  • Serve as the primary problem-solver for any non-standard situations.
  • Ensure all quality standards in this SOP are executed by the team and vendors.
  • Manage client communication during the final planning stages and on-site.

2. Client Manager (CM) / Personal Wedding Butler

The CM is dedicated to the couple's personal experience on the wedding day. Their goal is to ensure the couple feels supported, relaxed, and happy.

Responsibilities:

  • Act as the couple’s personal assistant throughout the entire event.
  • Manage the couple's personal schedule according to the rundown.
  • Brief the couple on processionals for the ceremony and reception.
  • Carry the "CM Bag" with essentials (water, tissues, flip-flops, first aid, etc.).
  • Act as the Area Manager for Photography and Videography, ensuring vendor SOPs are followed.
  • Maintain a calm, positive, and reassuring presence for the couple.

3. Area Manager (AM)

For large events, specific team members will be assigned as Area Managers to ensure focused control over key domains.

Responsibilities:

  • Oversee a specific area (e.g., Catering & Bar, Decorations, Entertainment).
  • Act as the direct supervisor for all vendors within their assigned area.
  • Ensure vendors in their area adhere to all quality standards, timelines, and on-site rules.
  • Report any issues within their area directly to the Project Manager.

Section B: The Event Lifecycle: From A to Z

This is our standard workflow for every project.

Phase 1: Booking & Confirmation

  1. Project Assignment: The Sales Manager (SM) will forward the booking details and assign a PM to the project, updating the Wedding Coordination (WC) document.
  2. Initiate Booking: The assigned PM must begin booking all required vendors and services within 48 hours.
    • All bookings must be made via the official 7agency.bookings@gmail.com email address. (Credentials for this account are managed securely and provided separately).
    • Use email labels to organize conversations by project.
  3. Booking Confirmation: Every booking requires a written confirmation from the vendor via email. Phone calls can be used for follow-up, but the email is the official record.
  4. Update the WC Document: The PM must diligently update the booking status for each item using the standard color codes: Green (Pre-booked) and Bright Green (Booking Confirmed).
  5. Information Gathering: The PM must review all client documents (contract, attachments, forms) in the client's folder to ensure bookings are accurate. Any unclear details must be clarified with the responsible SM.
  6. Handling Changes: If the SM notifies you of any changes (new items, cancellations), the PM must update the relevant vendor bookings within 48 hours.
  7. Final Re-confirmation: One month before the event date, the PM must contact every vendor to re-confirm all details. The status in the WC document must then be changed to "Booking Confirmed."
