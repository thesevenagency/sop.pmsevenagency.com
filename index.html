A Critical Update to Our Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

To all team members and vendor partners,

As our company evolves, it has become clear that our previous SOP no longer meets the high standards we set for ourselves and our clients. Due to past issues and outdated rules, we are implementing a necessary and comprehensive update.

This revised SOP is no longer just a guideline; it is Rule Number One.

Adherence is mandatory for every member of our internal team and for every vendor partner on every event, without exception. Its purpose is to ensure we all operate with the same level of professionalism, integrity, and excellence. We require everyone to study this document thoroughly.

Our Team: Roles & Responsibilities

Project Manager (PM)

The overall leader and main point of contact for the entire event. Responsible for the entire project lifecycle, from initial vendor booking to final event execution and reporting. Coordinates all team members and vendors. The primary problem-solver for any on-site issues. Ensures the entire event adheres to this SOP.

Meeting Manager (MM)

Conducts the pre-wedding meeting with clients upon their arrival in Bali. Responsible for finalizing all event details, preferences, and special requests with the client. Updates the official Wedding Report (WR) file with all final information, which serves as a key document for the PM's execution plan.

Client Manager (CM) / Personal Butler

Provides exceptionally personalized assistance to the couple throughout the wedding day. Acts as the couple's primary point of contact for personal needs, helping to manage their schedule and keep them comfortable and stress-free. For the duration of the event, the CM is assigned as the **Area Manager** for Photography and Videography, ensuring those vendors meet our quality standards.

Area Manager (AM)

A temporary role assigned by the PM for a specific event. An AM is responsible for quality control and smooth execution within a dedicated area (e.g., Catering, Decorations, Entertainment). They do not book vendors but ensure the booked vendors perform according to the SOP.

Head Manager (HPM) / Development Manager (DM) / Finance Manager (FM)

Senior management responsible for high-level oversight. The HPM assigns PMs to projects. The DM approves new vendors. The FM manages all financial matters, including vendor pricing and payments.

Core Principles for Every Wedding

1. Venue: Clean & Private

The ceremony area and visible surroundings must be completely clean. Team members must politely ask onlookers to move away during the ceremony.

2. Follow the Rundown

The PM and all AMs must follow the event timeline (rundown) exactly. Report any delays to the PM immediately.

3. Client Arrival

The client must not arrive before the time stated in the rundown. Before they arrive, everything must be 100% ready: decorations, team, music, etc.

4. Vendor Arrival & Payment

Vendors must arrive at their appointed time. If late, the PM will not pay them. The vendor must contact the Finance Manager to resolve payment.

5. Use Appointed Vendors

PMs must only book vendors from the official company list. For exceptions, get approval from the Head Manager.

The Event Lifecycle

Booking Protocol

  1. PM Assignment: After deposit, HPM assigns a PM.
  2. Booking via Email: PM books all vendors via `7agency.bookings@gmail.com`. No personal emails/WhatsApp for bookings.
  3. Vendor Confirmation: Vendor must reply to the official email to confirm. This is the official record.
  4. Final Confirmation: One month before the event, PM re-confirms all details with all vendors via the official email.

Making the Rundown (Event Timeline)

Big Weddings: The responsible PM must create the wedding rundown at least one month before the wedding date. Once created, the PM sends it to the Sales Manager, who will share it with the client.

Small Weddings: The PM must create the rundown after the pre-wedding meeting between the client and the Meeting Manager is complete.

Product & Price Knowledge

All PMs must know the company's packages. All vendors must be paid according to the official Nett Price List. No PM can approve a higher price without written approval from the FM.

Technical Meetings & Rehearsals

A technical meeting with all key vendors is mandatory for large weddings to review the plan. A separate rehearsal is held for the couple to practice the ceremony.

Client Updates

The Sales Manager (SM) or Meeting Manager (MM) will inform the PM of any changes after meeting with the client. The PM is responsible for updating all relevant vendors immediately.

Client Briefing

When the client arrives, the PM or CM must give a short (3-minute) briefing to explain the walking order and what will happen during the ceremony.

Atmosphere & Music

Background music is mandatory for all weddings to create a celebratory atmosphere. The music should be playing as the client approaches. A microphone must always be available and used for the ceremony.

Team & Vendor Standards

Appearance

  • Uniforms: All team members must look professional. For big events, the PM will choose one official uniform. For small events, a company t-shirt with dark pants is acceptable.
  • Vendors: Must wear their own professional uniform or all-black attire (plain black shirt, black pants, clean closed-toe shoes).
  • Grooming: Uniforms must be clean and ironed. Personal hygiene is mandatory.
  • Shoes: Clean, closed-toe shoes are required for all staff.
  • Accessories: Keep accessories minimal. Only approved hats are permitted.

Attitude & Conduct (Code of Conduct)

  • Be Professional: Be friendly, positive, and solution-oriented. Never say "it's not my job."
  • Be Discreet: Do not speak loudly. Keep personal bags out of sight. No personal mobile phone use in front of clients or guests.
  • No Alcohol or Smoking: Consuming alcohol is strictly forbidden. Smoking is only allowed in designated areas away from the event.
  • Staff Areas: All breaks and meals must happen in the designated staff area.
  • Crew Food Only: Do not eat any food prepared for clients or guests.

Anti-Fraud Policy

  • No Side Deals: All bookings, negotiations, and payments must go through official company channels. Attempting to arrange side deals is strictly forbidden.
  • Client Payments: If a client must make an on-site payment, the Finance Dept must first issue an official invoice and assign a PIC to accept the payment. Do not accept direct cash payments.
  • Consequences: Violating this policy will result in immediate termination and possible legal action.

Administration, Sanctions & Emergency Contacts

Vendor Management

  • New Permanent Vendors: Must be approved by management via a formal proposal.
  • One-Time Vendors: Require pre-approval from the HPM, with price approval from the FM. The PM is fully responsible for their quality and conduct.
  • No "In-House" Vendors: To avoid conflicts of interest, company team members are not permitted to act as vendors for any company events.

Sanctions & Penalties

For Internal Team: A 3-strike system applies. 1st Violation: Written Warning. 2nd Violation: Loss of payment for the event. 3rd Violation: Formal review with management, which may lead to termination.

For Vendors: 1st Violation: Written Warning. 2nd Violation: The vendor will not be paid for the event and must meet with the Head Manager to discuss the issue. Future work is not guaranteed.

Emergency Contacts

For urgent matters that cannot be resolved by the on-site PM, contact the appropriate department.
